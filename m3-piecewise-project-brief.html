🧮 WHAT IS A PIECEWISE FUNCTION? (Quick Recap)

A piecewise function uses different rules (formulas) for different parts of its domain. The standard notation looks like this:

f(x) = { Rule 1    if   condition 1        { Rule 2    if   condition 2        { Rule 3    if   condition 3 → Each rule is a formula (e.g. 2x + 1). Each condition is an inequality (e.g. x ≥ 0).

Key ideas you must demonstrate in this project:

ConceptWhat it means
Correct notationUse the curly brace { notation with conditions written as inequalities (e.g. x ≥ 0, x < 5).
Domain intervalsEach piece applies to a specific interval. Intervals must not overlap and must together cover the full domain.
Open & closed dotsClosed dot ● = value IS included. Open dot ○ = value is NOT included. At every boundary, exactly one piece owns that value.
Continuity checkA piecewise function is continuous at a boundary if both pieces give the same output there. If not, there is a jump discontinuity.
Graph featuresLabel intercepts, domain, range, and whether each piece is increasing or decreasing.
Real-world meaningEvery piece and every boundary must have a clear interpretation in your chosen real-world context.
📦 STEP 1 — CHOOSE YOUR SCENARIO

Choose one scenario below, or propose your own (teacher must approve by Day 1). Your scenario must have at least 3 pieces and be based on real data or a realistic situation.

Electricity Tariff
Linear pieces
Many electricity providers charge different rates per unit depending on how much you use. For example: 0.20 SAR/kWh for the first 6,000 kWh, then 0.30 SAR/kWh for the next 6,000, then 0.40 SAR/kWh beyond 12,000. Model C(x) where x is monthly consumption in kWh.
Taxi / Ride-hailing Fare
Linear pieces
A taxi charges a fixed pickup fee, then a per-km rate that changes after a certain distance (e.g. first 5 km at one rate, then a higher rate for longer trips). Model the cost C(x) where x is distance or time.
Income Tax Brackets
Linear pieces
Tax systems apply different percentage rates to different income bands. Look up a real tax bracket system (Saudi Arabia, UK, USA, or another country) and model the tax owed T(x) as a function of taxable income x.
Parking Fees
Linear pieces
Many car parks charge by the hour with a cap: e.g. 5 SAR/hour for the first 3 hours, then 3 SAR/hour up to 8 hours, then a flat daily rate after 8 hours. Model the fee F(x) where x is hours parked.
Delivery Pricing
Step / linear mix
An online shop charges different shipping rates based on order value: free above a threshold, flat fee in a middle range, per-kg charge for heavy or low-value orders. Model the shipping cost S(x).
Water Bill
Linear pieces
Municipal water bills often use tiered pricing. Model the monthly bill W(x) as a function of water consumed (in cubic metres) using a real or realistic tariff structure from any city.
Sports Performance Zone
Can mix types
A coach tracks whether an athlete is in the correct heart-rate zone. Zones change at specific thresholds (recovery, aerobic, anaerobic). Model the intensity level or calorie burn rate as a piecewise function of heart rate.
Custom Scenario
Any type
Any real piecewise situation you find (must have at least 3 pieces and be approved by Day 1). Include a source for your data.

📝 PART A — INVESTIGATION REPORT  (50 points)

Write in complete sentences. All mathematical work must be clearly shown. Submit as a PDF or shared Google Doc.

Section 1 — Scenario Introduction8 pts
Introduce your real-world scenario. Explain what it represents, where your data or rates came from, and why piecewise functions are the right tool to model it. Define what x represents and what f(x) represents, including real-world units.
Section 2 — The Piecewise Function22 pts
This is the mathematical core. You must include all of the following:
  1. The complete piecewise function in correct curly-brace notation with all pieces and conditions.
  2. For each linear piece: slope m, y-intercept b, and the formula f(x) = mx + b with m and b explained in context.
  3. The domain and range of the complete function.
  4. A continuity check at every boundary: show the calculation and state whether there is a jump or not.
  5. Evaluation of f(x) for at least 4 specific x-values (at least one per piece) with full contextual interpretation.
  6. The graph, with correct open/closed dots at every boundary, labelled axes, and labelled intercepts.
Section 3 — Real-World Interpretation12 pts
For each piece of your function:
  1. Explain what the rule (formula) means in plain English — why does the rate or value apply here?
  2. Explain what each boundary value means (e.g. “after 6,000 kWh, the rate increases because...”).
  3. Discuss whether the function is continuous or has jump discontinuities, and what that means practically (e.g. does a customer pay dramatically more for using just one extra unit?).
Section 4 — Extension: Absolute Value Connection8 pts
The absolute value function is itself a piecewise function:
f(x) = |x| = { x   if   x ≥ 0    and    −x   if   x < 0 }
Choose one of the following tasks:
  1. Show how your scenario (or a related one) could be modelled or approximated using an absolute value function. Write the function in vertex form f(x) = a|x − h| + k and identify and interpret the vertex.
  2. OR: Write a new real-world scenario (different from your main one) that is naturally modelled by an absolute value function. State the function, graph it, and interpret the vertex and both pieces.

💬 PART C — VIRTUAL MATH TALK  (20 points)

On Day 5 you will meet your teacher for a 10-minute one-on-one call. This is a conversation — come ready to discuss your work naturally. Sample questions:

“Read me your piecewise function out loud and explain what each piece means in your scenario.”
“Why is there a change at that specific boundary value? What happens in the real world at that point?”
“If x = [specific value], which piece do you use? What does f(x) equal, and what does it mean?”
“Is your function continuous at x = [boundary]? Show me the calculation.”
“What would change in your function if the rate in piece 2 increased by 10%?”
“How is your function related to an absolute value function? What would the vertex represent?”
Tip: Practise reading your piecewise notation out loud before the call. Have your graph visible on screen. Know which piece applies for any given x-value before the call starts.

🧮 Concepts & Procedures
Piecewise notation · Graph · Evaluation
Proficient
Carries out all procedures with precision and fluency. Correctly writes piecewise notation with all pieces, conditions, and curly-brace format. Accurately graphs all segments with correct open/closed endpoints, slopes, and labelled axes. Evaluates f(x) at multiple values across all pieces without errors. Makes clear connections between the algebraic rule, table of values, and graph.
Developing
Carries out procedures with some precision. Piecewise notation is mostly correct with one or two minor errors in domain boundaries or endpoint notation. Graph is largely accurate with small errors at one or two segments or endpoints. Evaluates most values correctly. Makes some connections between representations.
Emerging
Carries out procedures with limited precision. Piecewise notation is attempted but domains are incomplete or incorrect. Graph has several errors in segments or endpoint dots. Evaluation of f(x) contains errors or is incomplete. Makes limited connections between the algebraic rule and other representations.
Not Assessed
Did not demonstrate knowledge or understanding of piecewise procedures. No notation attempted, no graph produced, or no evaluations completed.
💬 Reasoning & Communication
Mathematical language · Notation · Justification
Proficient
Communicates using precise mathematical language throughout — domain, range, interval, boundary, continuity, open/closed dot — with correct symbolic notation and units throughout. Clearly explains what each piece of the function represents in real-world context with a detailed and organised mathematical argument. Makes valid conceptual connections and justifies all mathematical claims logically.
Developing
Uses some mathematical language and mostly correct notation. Explains the meaning of most pieces in context with units mostly present. Makes some valid arguments but justification of some claims is incomplete or partially organised.
Emerging
Uses limited mathematical language. Notation has significant errors or is missing. Explanation of real-world meaning is vague, incomplete, or missing units. Mathematical thinking is present but disorganised or hard to follow.
Not Assessed
Did not demonstrate mathematical language or mathematical thinking. No contextual explanation present.
🌍 Problem Solving & Modelling
Scenario selection · Model construction · Analysis
Proficient
Selects an appropriate and interesting real-world scenario and constructs a complete, accurate piecewise model with at least 3 pieces. Executes all mathematical procedures accurately — evaluations, continuity checks, absolute value extension. Provides in-depth written analysis explaining why piecewise modelling fits the situation, what each piece represents, and what happens at boundaries. Interprets outputs meaningfully in context.
Developing
Selects an appropriate scenario and builds a mostly complete model. Executes procedures with some minor computational errors. Evaluates the function at most input values correctly. Provides some analysis of the modelling choice with some real-world interpretation of boundaries and outputs.
Emerging
Selects a scenario but the model is incomplete or has conceptual errors. Executes procedures with minor conceptual and computational errors. Evaluation of f(x) contains errors. Analysis of the modelling choice is limited — pieces or boundaries are not explained in real-world terms.
Not Assessed
Did not provide analysis of problem solving strategies. No real-world scenario modelled, or no evaluation or analysis attempted.
